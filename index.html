Pre-Trip Checklist

Travel Tips

  • Respect Local Customs: Politeness and respect are highly valued in Japan. Bowing is common, but a simple nod is also appreciated.
  • Public Transport is Key: Tokyo's train and subway system is efficient and extensive. It's often the best way to get around.
  • Learn Basic Phrases: Even a few Japanese phrases like "Arigato" (Thank you) or "Sumimasen" (Excuse me/Sorry) can go a long way.
  • Cash is Still King (Sometimes): While cards are widely accepted, many smaller shops, restaurants, and vending machines still prefer cash.
  • Food Etiquette: Don't stick chopsticks upright in your rice. Slurping noodles is generally acceptable and can even be a sign of enjoyment!
  • Carry a Trash Bag: Public trash cans are rare in Japan. It's common to carry your trash until you find a bin (often near convenience stores or train stations).
  • Be Prepared for Weather Changes: October usually has pleasant weather, but sudden rain showers can occur. A light umbrella or rain jacket is always a good idea.
