Discover a vibrant underwater world at Shinagawa Aquarium, home to a diverse collection of marine life. Enjoy captivating dolphin and sea lion shows, marvel at colorful fish, and explore the impressive shark tank. It's a perfect engaging activity for families, especially with a young child, offering both entertainment and educational value.

Why Visit: Perfect for a relaxed first day after arrival, offering interactive exhibits and exciting animal shows that will delight both children and adults.

Morning: Ueno Park

Ueno Park is a sprawling public park known for its numerous museums, a zoo, and beautiful natural scenery. It's a fantastic spot for families, offering a blend of cultural enrichment and outdoor activities. You can enjoy a leisurely walk, visit the zoo, or explore one of the many cultural institutions within its grounds.

Why Visit: A cultural and natural oasis with diverse attractions, including museums, a zoo, and beautiful gardens, perfect for a family day out.

Practical Info:
  • Entry Fee: Park: Free, Zoo: Adults ¬•600, Children (0-12): Free
  • Estimated Cost: ¬•1,200
  • Best Time: Morning to early afternoon
  • Duration: 2-3 hours
  • Accessibility: Mostly flat with paved paths, stroller and wheelchair friendly
Tips:
  • What to Bring: Comfortable shoes, Snacks and water
  • What to Buy: Zoo souvenirs
  • Safety Notes: Keep children close in crowded areas, Be mindful of feeding times at the zoo

Late Morning: Tokyo National Museum

Located within Ueno Park, the Tokyo National Museum is Japan's oldest and largest museum, housing an extensive collection of art and artifacts from Japan and other Asian countries. It provides a fascinating insight into Japanese history and culture. While a 4-year-old might not grasp everything, the grand architecture and some visual exhibits can still be engaging.

Why Visit: Explore Japan's rich history and art through a vast collection of artifacts, offering a deeper understanding of the country's cultural heritage.

Practical Info:
  • Entry Fee: Adults: ¬•1000, Children (under 18): Free
  • Estimated Cost: ¬•2,000
  • Best Time: Late morning
  • Duration: 1.5-2 hours
  • Accessibility: Wheelchair and stroller accessible, elevators available
Tips:
  • What to Bring: Quiet activities for child (drawing pad)
  • What to Buy: Books, postcards, traditional crafts
  • Safety Notes: Be mindful of museum etiquette (no running, loud noises)
  • Customs/Etiquette: No touching exhibits
  • Booking Required: No
  • Photo Spots: Main building facade

Dive into an immersive digital art experience at teamLab Planets. This unique museum features large-scale, interactive art installations where you become part of the artwork. Children will be captivated by the flowing digital waterfalls, walking through koi ponds, and rooms filled with soft, giant spheres. It's a truly mesmerizing and sensory experience for the entire family.

Why Visit: An immersive digital art museum offering a unique, interactive, and visually stunning experience that appeals to all ages, igniting imagination and wonder.

Afternoon: Joypolis & DiverCity Tokyo Plaza

Experience thrilling indoor rides and arcade games at Tokyo Joypolis, a multi-story amusement park, followed by shopping and entertainment at DiverCity Tokyo Plaza. Outside DiverCity, marvel at the life-sized Unicorn Gundam statue, which offers an exciting light show. This area provides a lively mix of entertainment for the whole family.

Why Visit: A hub of futuristic entertainment with indoor rides, arcade games, and the iconic Gundam statue, perfect for an afternoon of family fun.

Practical Info:

  • Best Time: Afternoon to evening
  • Duration: 3-4 hours
  • Accessibility: Joypolis may have some restrictions for smaller children on certain rides; DiverCity is fully accessible.
Tips:
  • What to Bring: Comfortable shoes, Extra cash for games/souvenirs
  • What to Buy: Gundam models, Japanese fashion, snacks
  • Safety Notes: Supervise children on rides and in crowded areas
  • Customs/Etiquette: No outside food or drinks in Joypolis
